Moscow Mule

The Moscow Mule is a classic combination of vodka, ginger beer, and lime. Known for it's iconic copper mug, the drink's enduring popularity has left it as a mainstay in bars since the mid-20th century. Despite its name, the Moscow Mule was actually invented in Los Angeles as part of an early stateside marketing push for vodka, and the drink itself is considered an example of the Buck family of drinksâ€”those that include a spirit with citrus and ginger beer.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces vodka
  • 2 ounce lime juice, freshly squeezed
  • 3 ounces ginger beer, chilled
  • Garnish: lime wheel

Steps

  1. Fill a Moscow Mule mug (or highball glass) with ice, then add the vodka and lime juice.
  2. Top with the ginger beer.
  3. Garnish with a lime wheel.
